SECTION 05 — IP & PATENT PROTECTION

A Well-Constructed, Multi-Jurisdictional Moat Around a Novel Asset

The IP position surrounding Vepoloxamer is designed in layers — combining foundational composition-of-matter protection with multi-jurisdictional use-based coverage. Together, they provide meaningful commercial exclusivity globally, with additional regulatory exclusivity pathways available to extend effective protection into the 2040s.

LAYER 1

Composition of Matter Patent

Protects the purified poloxamer molecule itself — the most foundational and defensible form of pharmaceutical IP. Covers the compound regardless of which indication is pursued. Applicable globally through corresponding filings.

Expires: 2035
LAYER 2

Method of Use Patent Application — Filed by MUSC, Exclusively Licensed to LifeRaft

LifeRaft, through its research collaboration with the Medical University of South Carolina (MUSC), generated new preclinical data supporting Vepoloxamer's use as a systemic radioprotectant. MUSC has filed a Method of Use patent application on this work, covering the administration of Vepoloxamer (P188) to protect non-insulted cells from radiation damage — a specific, novel, and clinically meaningful application. LifeRaft holds an exclusive license to this patent application (and any patents issuing therefrom) under a formal license agreement with MUSC, including the right to sublicense.

Filed by: MUSC · Exclusively Licensed to: LifeRaft · Rights: Exclusive + Sublicense

Understanding the 2035 Timeline — An Opportunity, Not a Constraint

This asset includes one complete filed IND (EPIC) and a draft IMPD (Heart Failure), extensive clinical datasets, and a well-characterized manufacturing process. The path to Phase I/II initiation is measurably shorter than for a true pre-IND asset starting from scratch.

2026–2027

Lead Indication Selection & PoC Trial Design

Leverage the existing IND and retested API to design a lean proof-of-concept Phase I/II. STEMI or ischemic stroke as primary candidate; oral mucositis as parallel path. Existing PK/safety data from prior trials dramatically accelerates protocol design and FDA pre-IND meeting.

2027–2029

Phase I / Phase II Clinical Trials

Existing safety profile across ~4,000 patient-exposures de-risks Phase I substantially. Orphan Drug and Breakthrough Therapy pathways available for several indications.

2029–2032

Phase III / Pivotal Trials

Registration-enabling studies in lead indication. Priority review and early commercialization in lead indication while secondary indications advance.

2032–2040+

Approval, Launch & Extended Exclusivity

Patents issuing from method-of-use patent applications (US/EP/JP) + data exclusivity (5 yrs NCE / 7 yrs Orphan) + pediatric extensions can push effective exclusivity to 2040+.

Effective Exclusivity Can Extend Well Beyond 2035

The Composition of Matter patent is the floor, not the ceiling. Patents issuing from method-of-use patent applications (US, EP, JP), data exclusivity (including 7-year Orphan Drug protection available in SCD and DMD), and potential pediatric extensions mean a strategic acquirer who moves decisively could command effective market exclusivity well into the 2040s — capturing the full commercial window for a successful launch.
