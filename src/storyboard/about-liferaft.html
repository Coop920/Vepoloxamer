ABOUT LIFERAFT BIOSCIENCES

About LifeRaft Biosciences

LifeRaft Biosciences is a biopharmaceutical investment company headquartered in Charleston, South Carolina. LifeRaft was founded by a private investor who had followed the scientific and clinical development of poloxamer 188 through its prior sponsors, recognized the value of the asset following Savara's decision to wind down the program, and created LifeRaft as the vehicle to acquire it and continue its development. Vepoloxamer is the company's focus.

LifeRaft's focus is singular: advance Vepoloxamer toward a clinical proof of concept and position the asset for partnership with an organization that has the capital and clinical infrastructure to take it to approval.

Why LifeRaft Acquired Vepoloxamer

The founding investor had followed poloxamer 188 through its prior development programs and believed the Phase III failures reflected trial design and endpoint decisions rather than molecular biology failure. When Savara decided not to continue the program, LifeRaft acquired the asset — the Composition of Matter patent, the IND files, the clinical datasets, and the published science — and initiated a new preclinical program at MUSC to develop the radioprotection indication. The asset is now positioned for the next phase of development with a partner who has the capital and clinical infrastructure to take it forward.
