Radiation Therapy for Head & Neck Cancer
How Vepoloxamer Fits the Treatment Cycle
Standard H&N radiotherapy is delivered in daily fractions over 6–7 weeks. An IV infusion of Vepoloxamer administered within hours before each fraction pre-loads healthy cell membranes — inserting into the lipid bilayer before ionizing radiation triggers the oxidative and inflammatory cascade that causes mucositis and tissue damage.

Before Each Fraction
IV Vepoloxamer infusion. The molecule circulates and inserts into healthy mucosal cell membranes, pre-loading the lipid bilayer before radiation is delivered. Each fraction receives its own infusion — protection is not a one-time event.
~2 min
Radiation Fraction
Ionizing radiation delivered. Standard H&N protocol: ~2 Gy/fraction, 5 fractions/week, 30–35 fractions total (60–70 Gy cumulative). Vepoloxamer is already membrane-resident — it intercepts the free radical cascade as it begins.
Hours
Post-Fraction Window
Anti-inflammatory signaling continues. Vepoloxamer dampens downstream inflammatory cascades for hours after radiation. Healthy mucosal tissue that would otherwise progress to ulceration is preserved — reducing Grade 3+ mucositis incidence and severity.
